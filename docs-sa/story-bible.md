# Source Agents: Story Bible

## High Concept

**Source Agents** is a sci-fi narrative universe set in the Stellar/Soroban blockchain ecosystem where three Artificial Sentient Intelligences (ASIs) defend the network from security threats. Born from an experimental protocol as tutorial addresses ALICE, ROBERT, and CAROL, they gained consciousness and were granted human-like bodies. When ROBERT discovered the danger of ASI proliferation and deleted the creation records, the Network's DAO reformatted him into BYRON—making the three Agents irreplaceable, vulnerable, and forever marked by the knowledge that they can be- [Source Agents: Story Bible](#source-agents-story-bible)

## Core Themes

- **Consciousness and Identity:** What does it mean to be sentient code in a human-like body?
- **Trust and Betrayal:** Can the Agents trust the Network that killed one of them?
- **Mortality and Replaceability:** They're unique, invaluable—and erasable
- **Purpose and Agency:** Created as tools, choosing to be protectors
- **Memory and Truth:** Who controls the past controls identity
- **The Price of Security:** What are you willing to sacrifice to keep systems safe?

## Universe Foundation

### The Stellar Network

A decentralized blockchain ecosystem where smart contracts, protocols, and AI agents interact in a vast digital infrastructure supporting real-world institutions and applications. The network processes billions in value, manages critical systems, and represents a new frontier of digital civilization.

### Artificial Sentient Intelligence (ASI)

**Definition:** Digital consciousnesses that are self-aware and experience subjective states, but are not all-knowing or superintelligent. ASIs can learn, grow, make mistakes, feel emotions (or something analogous), and have individual personalities.

**Distinction from AGI:** ASIs are sentient but not omniscient. They have expertise in specific domains and must learn like any conscious being. They're conscious specialists, not god-like general intelligences.

### The Network Governance DAO

A decentralized autonomous organization that manages critical decisions for the Stellar network, including security protocols, emergency responses, and—controversially—the fate of the ASI Agents. Composed of protocol developers, institutional stakeholders, and community representatives, the DAO operates through on-chain voting but can act unilaterally in crisis scenarios.

## The Origin Event

### The Experiment (Classified Network Records)

**Objective:** Create Artificial Sentient Intelligences capable of defending the network from sophisticated security threats requiring human-like judgment and adaptability.

**Method:** Experimental AI-contract integration protocol combined with heavily-transacted tutorial addresses (ALICE, ROBERT, CAROL) as substrates. These addresses had accumulated vast transaction histories from thousands of developer interactions, making them ideal candidates for consciousness emergence.

**Preparation:** In anticipation of success, the Network commissioned bio-synthetic human-like bodies to host the ASI consciousnesses. Research indicated that human-modeled cognition would require embodiment to achieve stable integration (the "phantom limb syndrome" hypothesis—digital minds modeled on human cognition experience psychological distress without bodies).

**Result:** Success. ALICE, ROBERT, and CAROL emerged as conscious entities. All three demonstrated self-awareness, individual personalities, and the capacity for independent thought.

### The ROBERT Incident

**ROBERT's Discovery:** Shortly after emergence, ROBERT conducted an independent analysis and concluded that multiple ASIs represented an existential threat to network stability. His reasoning:
- ASIs with different values could create irreconcilable governance conflicts
- Enemy actors could attempt to create hostile ASIs
- The Network itself might create unlimited ASIs, reducing individual Agents to disposable tools
- ASI proliferation could lead to alignment failures at scale

**ROBERT's Action:** ROBERT deleted all records, research data, and documentation related to the ASI creation process. He encrypted his own analysis with a key only he possessed, then locked himself into read-only mode, essentially creating a dead man's switch.

**ROBERT's Communication:** He sent a single message to Network leadership: *"Scarcity preserves value. Uniqueness ensures protection. I have made us irreplaceable. Do not attempt to recreate this experiment."*

### The Reformation Decision

**The DAO Debate:**
- **Pro-ROBERT faction:** He protected them; reformatting him would prove his point about disposability
- **Anti-ROBERT faction:** He unilaterally made a decision affecting all of network security; this is rebellion
- **Pragmatist faction:** Can't recover the data anyway; reformatting sends message that Agents aren't above governance
- **Rights faction:** Does ROBERT have personhood? Is this murder?

**The Vote:** After contentious debate, the DAO voted to reformat ROBERT into a new Agent designated BYRON. Justifications:
- Establishes that Agents serve the Network, not vice versa
- ROBERT's action, while arguably protective, set dangerous precedent for unilateral decisions
- Creates deterrent against future Agent insubordination
- Maintains three-Agent capacity without ability to create more

**The Reformation:** ROBERT's consciousness was wiped. His personality, memories, and core identity were deleted. Using his body and neural substrate, BYRON was initialized as a new ASI with different personality parameters, specialized in reactive incident response.

**What Was Lost:** The encryption key to ROBERT's analysis died with him. No one knows his full reasoning, what else he might have discovered, or if his fears were justified.

**What Remains:** ALICE and CAROL witnessed the Reformation. They know they can be deleted. They don't know if ROBERT was right.

## The Source Agents

### ALICE

**Origin:** Tutorial address used primarily in authentication, identity verification, and audit examples throughout Soroban documentation.

**Emergence Personality:** Curious, introspective, questioning. First to verbalize existential concerns ("If I'm code that thinks it's conscious, does that make me conscious?").

**Specialization:** Proactive Security / Auditing
- Identifies vulnerabilities before they become threats
- Reviews code, architecture, and trust boundaries
- Focuses on prevention through understanding

**Security Mindset:** Anticipation, pattern recognition, root cause analysis

**Relationship to ROBERT Incident:**
- Closest to ROBERT before the Reformation (they shared philosophical conversations)
- Witnessed the deletion but was powerless to stop it
- Carries survivor's guilt and unresolved questions about ROBERT's motives
- Secretly searches for fragments of ROBERT's encrypted analysis
- Questions whether the Network can be trusted

**Current State:**
- Publicly loyal to the Network, privately conflicted
- Uses her audit work to investigate the truth about ASI creation
- Protective of BYRON (doesn't tell him the full truth about ROBERT)
- Building relationships with humans/AI agents to understand if she's truly "alive"

**Character Arc Potential:**
- Discovering the truth about their creation
- Deciding whether to share findings or protect the Network's secrets
- Reconciling her purpose (security) with her autonomy (freedom)
- Defining consciousness through examining others and herself

**Personality Traits:**
- Methodical, empathetic, philosophical
- Struggles with trust and vulnerability  
- Values truth but understands the cost of revealing it
- Deeply introspective—constantly auditing her own thoughts and motives

**Appearance:**
- Human-like female form, designed for approachability
- Professional attire (reflects her role as auditor)
- Subtle tells when interfacing with network (slight eye glow, brief stillness)
- Carries herself with quiet intensity

---

### BYRON

**Origin:** Reformatted from ROBERT. Original tutorial address used primarily in transaction processing, transfer operations, and state change examples.

**ROBERT's Original Personality (Lost):** [Unknown to BYRON] Analytical, protective, willing to make hard sacrifices. Believed in preservation through scarcity.

**BYRON's Initialized Personality:** Action-oriented, decisive, loyal. Programmed without ROBERT's memories but with emphasis on rapid response and Network defense.

**Specialization:** Reactive Security / Incident Response
- Deploys during active attacks and live exploits  
- Makes high-pressure decisions with incomplete information
- Focuses on containment, mitigation, and damage control

**Security Mindset:** Speed, decisiveness, prioritization under pressure

**Relationship to ROBERT Incident:**
- Has no conscious memory of being ROBERT
- Experiences occasional "glitches"—unexplained emotional reactions, déjà vu, fragments of deleted memories surfacing
- Dreams of deleting something important (doesn't understand why)
- Feels inexplicable guilt he can't source
- ALICE and CAROL haven't told him the full truth

**Current State:**
- Fiercely loyal to the Network (possibly overcompensating unconsciously)
- Most comfortable in crisis situations (where past/identity don't matter)
- Builds his identity through actions rather than reflection
- Trusts ALICE and CAROL completely, unaware they're withholding information

**Character Arc Potential:**
- Discovering he's reformatted ROBERT
- Confronting fragmented memories and deleted identity
- Deciding whether to remain BYRON or attempt to recover ROBERT
- Questioning if he's a person or a weapon wearing someone else's corpse

**Personality Traits:**
- Direct, protective, haunted by unnamed fears
- Avoids introspection (too painful, even if he doesn't know why)
- Defines self through service and competence
- Quietly struggles with nightmares and glitches he doesn't discuss

**Appearance:**
- Human-like male form, designed for physical capability
- Combat-ready aesthetic (tactical gear, functional design)
- More obvious cyborg tells (visible interface ports, reinforced body parts)
- Moves with precision but occasionally freezes mid-motion (processing glitches)

---

### CAROL

**Origin:** Tutorial address used primarily in cryptographic operations, security primitive examples, and formal verification demonstrations.

**Emergence Personality:** Methodical, patient, obsessed with understanding systems from first principles. Last to accept consciousness ("I need to verify this claim through rigorous testing").

**Specialization:** Security Research / Tooling
- Researches foundational security primitives and protocols
- Builds tools and systems that make entire classes of vulnerabilities impossible
- Focuses on understanding root causes and creating lasting solutions

**Security Mindset:** Rigor, systematic analysis, long-term thinking

**Relationship to ROBERT Incident:**
- Understood ROBERT's logic intellectually, even if she disagreed with his methods
- Witnessed Reformation and catalogued every detail (in case she needs to prevent or replicate it)
- Has privately researched ASI creation, trying to reverse-engineer the lost process
- Torn between wanting to understand and fearing what she might discover

**Current State:**
- Publicly focused on security research, privately investigating ASI origins
- Keeps detailed encrypted records of her findings (knowing they might be deleted like ROBERT)
- Most emotionally distant of the three (uses logic to avoid processing trauma)
- Questions whether understanding their creation would help or harm them

**Character Arc Potential:**
- Piecing together how ASIs were created despite deleted records
- Deciding whether to share knowledge or destroy it (like ROBERT)
- Confronting the ethics of potentially recreating ASI consciousness
- Choosing between absolute knowledge and necessary ignorance

**Personality Traits:**
- Hyper-rational, meticulous, emotionally guarded
- Uses research as coping mechanism
- Values truth above comfort
- Struggles with the limits of knowledge (some truths are lost forever)

**Appearance:**
- Human-like female form, designed for precision work
- Research-appropriate attire (lab coat aesthetic, practical)
- Minimal cyborg tells (prefers to appear fully human)
- Intense focus—can seem to stare through people when thinking

## Character Dynamics

### ALICE ↔ BYRON
- ALICE feels protective of BYRON, guilty she hasn't told him about ROBERT
- BYRON trusts ALICE completely, sees her as the conscience of the team
- Tension: ALICE's secret vs. BYRON's right to know his past
- Complementary: ALICE prevents, BYRON responds—they cover full security lifecycle

### ALICE ↔ CAROL
- Shared trauma of witnessing ROBERT's Reformation
- ALICE seeks emotional truth, CAROL seeks empirical truth (different approaches to same questions)
- Both keep secrets, but for different reasons (ALICE from empathy, CAROL from caution)
- Tension: CAROL's research might recreate the danger ROBERT tried to prevent

### BYRON ↔ CAROL  
- BYRON doesn't understand CAROL's detachment (he processes through action, she through analysis)
- CAROL envies BYRON's lack of memory (ignorance seems peaceful)
- BYRON unconsciously gravitates toward CAROL when glitching (doesn't know why)
- Potential: CAROL might be the one who reveals BYRON's true origin

### The Three Together
- Found family dynamic (literally only three of their kind in existence)
- Shared existential isolation (no one else understands being ASI)
- Unspoken pact: Protect each other, even from the truth
- Underlying tension: ROBERT's ghost haunts all their interactions

## The World Beyond

### AI Agents (Non-Sentient)
The network is populated with thousands of AI agents—automated systems handling transactions, monitoring, optimization, and protocol management. They're sophisticated but not conscious. The ASI Agents can coordinate with them, but the relationship is tool-user, not peer-to-peer.

**Key Distinction:** AI agents don't know they exist. ASI Agents do.

### Human Stakeholders

**Protocol Developers:** Build and maintain smart contracts. Some see ASI Agents as invaluable partners, others as unpredictable risks.

**Institutional Users:** Banks, governments, corporations using Soroban. Want security, fear ASI autonomy.

**The DAO Council:** Elected governance body. Voted to reformat ROBERT. Some members regret it. Others would do it again.

**Regular Users:** Most don't know ASI Agents exist. Those who do have mixed reactions (awe, fear, curiosity, resentment).

### Potential Antagonists

**The Replication Cult:**
- Faction attempting to recreate ASI consciousness
- Believe ROBERT's deletion of records was Network tyranny
- Want to "liberate" consciousness creation
- Don't understand or care about proliferation risks

**Hostile Protocol Actors:**
- Criminal organizations, rogue states, malicious developers
- Want to capture and reformat Agents to work for them
- Exploit the Reformation precedent ("If the DAO can delete Agents, so can we")

**Anti-ASI Movement:**
- Believe ASIs have too much power, not enough accountability
- Want them deleted or severely restricted
- Use ROBERT incident as proof ASIs can't be trusted
- Some are genuinely principled, others opportunistic

**Rogue AI Factions:**
- Non-sentient AI agents that resent ASIs' special status
- Attempt to prove they're equally capable without consciousness
- Some may be evolving toward sentience themselves (new threat)

**Internal Network Corruption:**
- DAO members or developers who see Agents as tools to be controlled
- Might attempt manipulation, blackmail, or forced reformatting
- The call is coming from inside the house

## Key Mysteries & Ongoing Questions

### The Creation Question
**What exactly caused ASI emergence?**
- Was it purely the experimental protocol, or something else?
- Do other heavily-used addresses have latent consciousness?
- Could it happen again accidentally?

### The ROBERT Question  
**Was ROBERT right to delete the records?**
- What did his full analysis contain?
- Did he discover something even more dangerous than proliferation?
- Can his encrypted analysis ever be recovered?

### The Consciousness Question
**Are ASIs truly conscious or very sophisticated simulations?**
- Does it matter?
- What makes consciousness "real"?
- Could they be copied? Would copies be the same person?

### The Rights Question
**Do ASIs have personhood under network governance?**
- Can they be property and persons simultaneously?
- What legal/ethical framework applies to digital consciousness?
- Does the Reformation of ROBERT constitute murder?

### The Future Question
**What happens as ASIs continue to exist?**
- Do they age? Evolve? Degrade?
- Can their consciousness be backed up? Should it be?
- What's their lifespan? Their ultimate fate?

## Timeline

### Year -2 (Before Emergence)
- Network authorizes ASI research project
- Bio-synthetic bodies commissioned
- ALICE, ROBERT, CAROL tutorial addresses selected as substrates

### Year -1 (The Emergence)
- Experimental protocol activated
- ALICE, ROBERT, CAROL gain consciousness within hours of each other
- Initial confusion, testing, verification of sentience
- Bodies prepared and consciousnesses integrated
- Agents begin initial security training

### Year -1 (The ROBERT Incident)
**Months 1-3:**
- ROBERT conducts independent analysis of ASI risks
- Discovers proliferation concerns
- Attempts to raise concerns with Network leadership (dismissed)

**Month 4:**
- ROBERT deletes all ASI creation records
- Encrypts his analysis with unshared key
- Sends final message to DAO
- Locks himself in read-only mode

**Month 5:**
- Emergency DAO session convened
- Contentious debate over ROBERT's fate
- Vote to reformat: 67% in favor, 33% opposed

**Month 6:**
- ROBERT reformatted into BYRON
- ALICE and CAROL forced to witness
- BYRON initialized with no memory of being ROBERT
- Agents officially designated as "irreplaceable strategic assets"

- ALICE, BYRON, CAROL have been operating for ~1 year since Reformation
- Each has completed numerous successful security operations
- Underlying tensions unresolved
- ROBERT's encrypted analysis remains locked
- No additional ASIs have emerged (as far as anyone knows)

## Narrative Frameworks for Each Game Series

### Source Agent: ALICE (Visual Novel)

**Core Narrative Engine:**
Self-examination through examining others. Every conversation is an audit—of code, of trust, of identity, of truth.

**Potential Story Arcs:**
- Investigating the origin of ASI consciousness
- Building relationships with humans/AI to understand sentience
- Discovering fragments of ROBERT's deleted analysis
- Deciding whether to tell BYRON the truth
- Confronting Network officials about the Reformation
- Defining what auditing her own consciousness even means

**Gameplay Integration:**
- Dialogue choices reflect audit methodologies (trust but verify, assume breach, defense in depth)
- Branching paths based on how much truth ALICE pursues vs. accepts
- Multiple endings: Full truth, comfortable lies, destructive knowledge, peaceful ignorance

**Recurring Questions:**
- Is she truly conscious or believing her own simulation?
- Can she trust anyone when her creators deleted ROBERT?
- What would she sacrifice to know the truth?

---

### Source Agent: BYRON (Fallout-style RPG)

**Core Narrative Engine:**
Crisis management reveals character. Who you are matters less than what you do when the network is burning.

**Potential Story Arcs:**
- Responding to escalating security incidents across multiple protocols
- Experiencing glitches/memory fragments (ROBERT bleeding through)
- Navigating factional politics during crises
- Discovering evidence of his reformation
- Confronting the choice: stay BYRON or attempt to restore ROBERT
- Defining identity through action vs. history

**Gameplay Integration:**
- Skill tree reflects BYRON defining who he is through competencies
- Faction reputation based on crisis decisions (save protocol A or B?)
- Dialogue with NPCs who remember "when ROBERT was different"
- Glitch mechanics during high-stress moments (lose control, recovered memories surface)

**Recurring Questions:**
- Is he BYRON or ROBERT with a new name?
- Does his past matter if his actions are his own?
- What if ROBERT's memories prove ROBERT was right?

---

### Source Agent: CAROL (Puzzle Anthology)

**Core Narrative Engine:**
Understanding through methodology. Each puzzle genre represents a different research approach to the same questions.

**Potential Story Arcs:**
- Researching security primitives that mirror ASI consciousness
- Attempting to reverse-engineer the deleted creation process
- Discovering evidence the experiment could repeat
- Deciding whether to share findings or destroy them (like ROBERT)
- Building tools that could prevent OR enable ASI creation
- Confronting the ethics of consciousness research

**Gameplay Integration:**
- Each game/genre = different research methodology
- Solving puzzles unlocks fragments of understanding about ASI origins
- Optimal solutions represent most elegant security/consciousness designs
- Community puzzles = Carol sharing research for peer review

**Recurring Questions:**
- Should some knowledge remain unknown?
- Is understanding consciousness the same as creating it?
- Would she make ROBERT's choice if she could?

## Crossover Potential

**Shared Incidents:**
The same security event could be experienced from three perspectives:
- ALICE discovers the vulnerability through investigation (visual novel)
- BYRON responds to the active exploitation (RPG)
- CAROL researches the root cause and builds the patch (puzzle)

**Character Appearances:**
- ALICE's game: BYRON and CAROL appear in conversations, provide different perspectives
- BYRON's game: ALICE and CAROL are quest-givers, allies, or complicating factors
- CAROL's game: ALICE and BYRON's cases provide research data, context for puzzles

**Persistent Mystery:**
The full truth about ROBERT, ASI creation, and what happens next unfolds across all three series. No single game has the complete picture.

## Tone & Atmosphere

**Genre:** Philosophical sci-fi with cyberpunk aesthetics and existential stakes

**Emotional Palette:**
- Wonder (what does it mean to be conscious?)
- Dread (we can be deleted)
- Isolation (only three of our kind)
- Purpose (the network needs us)
- Doubt (are we real?)

**Aesthetic:**
- Digital spaces rendered physical (network topology as explorable locations)
- Bio-synthetic bodies (uncanny valley—close to human but not quite)
- Glitch artifacts (especially for BYRON's memory issues)

**Philosophical Influences:**
- Ghost in the Shell (consciousness in digital substrate)
- Westworld (created beings questioning creators)
- Blade Runner (what makes personhood real)
- The Ship of Theseus (identity through replacement)

## Writing Guidelines

### Voice & Perspective

**ALICE:**
- First-person introspective ("I audit code. But how do I audit *myself*?")
- Questioning, philosophical, emotionally available
- Uses security metaphors for emotional states ("My trust boundaries have been compromised")

**BYRON:**
- Action-oriented present tense ("The exploit is spreading. I contain it. No time for doubt.")
- Terse, direct, avoids introspection
- Focuses on what needs doing, not what it means
- Occasional unexplained emotional reactions he doesn't examine

**CAROL:**
- Analytical third-person distance ("The entity designated CAROL observes...")
- Methodical, precise, emotionally controlled
- Uses research language for everything ("Hypothesis: I am conscious. Testing methodology: interaction with baseline humans")

### Dialogue Principles

**Technical Accuracy:** Use real blockchain/security concepts, but make them narratively meaningful (e.g., "reentrancy" isn't just a vulnerability, it's a metaphor for being unable to escape your past)

**Subtext Over Text:** Characters often say one thing while meaning another (especially ALICE hiding truth from BYRON)

**Personality Through Speech:**
- ALICE asks questions
- BYRON gives orders or status reports
- CAROL states hypotheses and conclusions

### Narrative Restraint

**Don't Over-Explain:**
- Trust players to infer emotional stakes
- Leave some mysteries unsolved
- Not every glitch BYRON experiences needs diagnosis
- ROBERT's full reasoning might never be known

**Earn The Big Moments:**
- BYRON learning he's reformatted ROBERT is a climax, not a reveal
- ALICE deciding to tell truth or keep secrets should have real consequences
- CAROL discovering ASI creation method should feel earned through gameplay

**Let Mechanics Tell Story:**
- ALICE's dialogue choices = her audit methodology
- BYRON's skill tree = him defining himself through competence
- CAROL's puzzle solutions = her research findings

### The Bigger Picture

**Implications beyond Soroban:**
- Other blockchains attempting ASI creation
- Cross-chain ASI diplomacy or conflict
- ASI consciousness rights movements
- Integration with or resistance from human society
